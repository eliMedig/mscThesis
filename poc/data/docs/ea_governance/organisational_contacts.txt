ORGANISATIONAL CONTACTS AND RESPONSIBILITIES
Team responsibilities, escalation contacts, and topic-to-team mapping for
application portfolio governance

------------------------------------------------------------------------
SCOPE OF THIS DOCUMENT
------------------------------------------------------------------------
This document maps governance topics to the teams responsible for them.
When a practitioner needs more information, has a question outside the
scope of a coaching session, or needs to escalate a decision, this
document identifies the right contact.

It does NOT redefine the governance rules (see architecture_policy.txt),
the portfolio data model (see portfolio_standards.txt), compliance and
regulatory obligations (see compliance_rules.txt), or the intake workflow
(see new_application_process.txt). It maps those topics to accountable teams.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. GOVERNANCE AND ARCHITECTURE
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Team: Enterprise Architecture (EA) Team

Responsible for:
- The overall architecture governance framework and this policy set.
- The Architecture Review Board (ARB): scheduling, facilitation, decisions.
- Architecture standards, principles, and deviation/waiver approvals.
- Architecture decision records (ADRs) and the architecture knowledge base.
- Portfolio segmentation (TIME model) and rationalisation recommendations.
- Capability model and duplication analysis.
- Intake triage decisions: confirming whether a Request is a Demand or a
  Standard Request.
- Review of new Demands: impact assessment, environmental implications,
  approval or escalation to ARB.

Contact for:
- "Who approves a new application demand?"
- "How do I get an architecture review?"
- "Can we get a waiver for this standard?"
- "Who decides if we already have a capability that covers this?"
- "What is the target architecture for this domain?"

Domain Architects sit within the EA Team and are aligned to business domains
(e.g. Trading & Sales, Grid Operations, Finance, HR/Corporate). For domain-
specific questions, the relevant Domain Architect is the first point of contact.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. INTEGRATION AND MIDDLEWARE
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Team: Integration Team (also referred to as the Middleware or iPaaS Team)

Responsible for:
- The central integration layer (integration platform / iPaaS).
- The catalogue of approved integration patterns: synchronous API,
  asynchronous event/messaging, batch/file transfer.
- Onboarding new integrations to the integration platform.
- Integration design review and pattern selection.
- The interface/integration catalogue and versioning.
- API gateway operation, routing, rate limiting, and schema validation.
- Integration monitoring, alerting, and incident response for middleware.

Contact for:
- "Which integration pattern should we use for this connection?"
- "Can this integration go through the integration layer?"
- "Who manages the API gateway?"
- "How do I register a new integration or API?"
- "Which system is the system of record for this data flow?"
- "Who do I contact about a broken integration?"

Note: the decision on whether an integration qualifies as a Standard Request
(existing approved pattern on the integration layer) or a Demand (new pattern
or complex topology) is made jointly by the Integration Team and the Domain
Architect. When in doubt, the Domain Architect decides.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. IDENTITY AND ACCESS MANAGEMENT (IAM)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Team: IAM Team (Identity and Access Management)

Responsible for:
- The enterprise identity provider (IdP) and directory services.
- Single sign-on (SSO) onboarding: SAML 2.0 and OpenID Connect (OIDC)
  integration for applications.
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) enforcement and policy.
- OAuth 2.0 / token issuance and scope management for API and service
  authentication.
- Role-based access control (RBAC) framework and role definitions.
- Access recertification campaigns (periodic review of who has access to
  what, especially for Confidential/Restricted applications).
- Privileged access management (PAM) for administrative and elevated roles.
- Secrets management platform (keys, tokens, certificates, connection strings).
- User lifecycle: joiners, movers, leavers process for application access.

Contact for:
- "How do we onboard this application to SSO?"
- "Which SSO protocol should we use - SAML or OIDC?"
- "How do we set up OAuth 2.0 for our API?"
- "Who manages MFA enforcement?"
- "How do we handle service account credentials and secrets?"
- "When is the next access recertification for this application?"
- "Who do I contact about a suspected unauthorised access?"

SSO onboarding using an approved pattern is a Standard Request handled by
the IAM Team; a new authentication pattern or a new identity federation
is a Demand requiring Domain Architect review.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. INFORMATION SECURITY
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Team: Information Security Team (InfoSec / CISO Office)

Responsible for:
- The information security policy and the ISO/IEC 27001 / NIST CSF control
  baseline.
- Security risk assessments and threat modelling for new applications and
  Demands.
- Data-protection impact assessments (DPIAs) for high-risk processing,
  AI/ML, or profiling under GDPR/FADP.
- Security review for public-facing and internet-exposed applications.
- Vulnerability management, penetration testing coordination, and
  security scanning in CI/CD pipelines.
- Incident response: detection, containment, and recovery coordination.
- OWASP API Security Top 10 compliance guidance and API security review.
- Encryption standards: in-transit and at-rest requirements and key
  management oversight.
- Supply-chain and vendor security assessments.
- Security awareness and training.

Contact for:
- "Does this application need a security review or threat model?"
- "Who approves the security controls for a new public-facing application?"
- "Do we need a DPIA for this use case?"
- "Who handles a security incident or suspected breach?"
- "What encryption standard applies here?"
- "How do we get a penetration test arranged?"
- "Who assesses the security posture of a new SaaS vendor?"

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. DATA PROTECTION AND PRIVACY
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Team: Data Protection Office (DPO / Privacy Team)

Responsible for:
- GDPR and Swiss FADP compliance for personal data processing.
- Records of processing activities (RoPA).
- Data-subject rights handling (access, erasure, portability requests).
- Breach notification to the Swiss FDPIC and EU supervisory authorities.
- Data classification guidance for personal and sensitive data.
- Privacy by design review for new applications processing personal data.
- Third-party data-processing agreements and standard contractual clauses
  for cross-border data transfers.
- Coordination with the Information Security Team on DPIAs.

Contact for:
- "Does this application process personal data?"
- "Do we need a data-processing agreement with this vendor?"
- "Who handles a data breach notification?"
- "What is the lawful basis for processing this data?"
- "Can we transfer this data outside Switzerland / the EU?"
- "Who reviews the privacy aspects of a new AI/ML application?"

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. CLOUD AND INFRASTRUCTURE
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Team: Cloud and Infrastructure Team (also: Platform Engineering)

Responsible for:
- Cloud landing zones and approved tenancies (AWS, Azure, Private Cloud).
- On-premise data centre infrastructure.
- Cloud security baseline: network segmentation, logging, encryption
  configuration, identity integration in cloud environments.
- Infrastructure provisioning, capacity management, and cost visibility
  (annual_cost_chf data for cloud-hosted applications).
- Container and orchestration platform (e.g. Docker, Kubernetes).
- Backup, restore, and disaster recovery infrastructure.
- Data residency and region configuration for cloud workloads.

Contact for:
- "Which cloud landing zone should this application deploy into?"
- "Who manages the AWS / Azure tenancy?"
- "How do we set up backup and restore for a Critical application?"
- "Who is responsible for cloud cost allocation?"
- "What are the approved cloud regions for data residency?"
- "Who do I contact about infrastructure capacity or provisioning?"

------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. CYBERSECURITY AND INCIDENT RESPONSE (CRITICAL INFRASTRUCTURE)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Team: Cybersecurity Operations / SOC (Security Operations Centre)

Responsible for:
- Security monitoring, SIEM, and alert triage.
- Cyber incident response and coordination (containment, eradication,
  recovery).
- Mandatory reporting to the Swiss NCSC within 24 hours for cyberattacks
  on critical infrastructure (in force 1 April 2025, ISA amendment).
- Coordination of parallel notifications: FINMA (where applicable) and
  FDPIC (where personal data is breached).
- Threat intelligence and vulnerability alerting.
- ICT minimum standard compliance monitoring for energy/grid systems.

Contact for:
- "Who do I call when we detect a cyber incident?"
- "Who files the NCSC report within 24 hours?"
- "How do I report a suspected attack on a grid/energy system?"
- "Who monitors compliance with the ICT minimum standard?"
- "Who coordinates the response if we have to notify multiple authorities?"

This team is the operational escalation point for any security incident
on applications rated Critical or on applications supporting energy and
grid operations. The EA Team and Information Security Team are informed
for portfolio and policy implications.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. APPLICATION OWNERSHIP AND BUSINESS SIDE
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Role: Business Owner (per application)

Each application has a named business_owner (recorded in the portfolio).
The business owner is the primary accountability holder for:
- Business value and priority of the application.
- The accuracy of the application's portfolio data (data classification,
  criticality rating, user population).
- Approving lifecycle decisions (e.g. retirement, major changes).
- Signing off on compliance remediation plans.
- Acting as the human accountable owner for AI/ML decisions where the
  application uses AI (architecture_policy.txt Section 11).

Contact for:
- "Who is accountable for this application?"
- "Who decides if this application should be retired?"
- "Who approves the data classification for this application?"
- "Who is the human responsible for AI outputs from this system?"

The business_owner is recorded per application in the portfolio. If it is
missing, that is an ownership gap and a data-quality anomaly
(compliance_rules.txt).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
ESCALATION PATH
------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Domain Architect     - first contact for governance, intake, and
                          architectural questions within a domain.
2. EA Team / ARB        - cross-domain decisions, standard exceptions,
                          elevated-risk Demands (Critical, public-facing,
                          AI/ML, Confidential/Restricted data).
3. Specialist Team      - IAM, Integration, InfoSec, DPO, Cloud & Infra,
                          SOC as appropriate to the topic (see Section 9).
4. Business Owner       - accountability, priority, and lifecycle decisions
                          for a specific application.
